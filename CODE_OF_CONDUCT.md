# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want Socializ to be a welcoming, respectful, and useful community for everyone. Contributors and maintainers are expected to act professionally, assume good faith, and make room for different levels of experience and perspective.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Explain disagreement with evidence and focus on the work, not the person.
- Welcome questions and help newcomers find a path forward.
- Protect private information and do not share secrets, credentials, or personal data.
- Respect the project's license and the work of other contributors.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, discrimination, intimidation, threats, personal attacks, sexualized comments, doxxing, trolling, and deliberate disruption are not acceptable. Public or private communication that violates these standards may be acted on.

## Enforcement

Report a conduct concern privately to the repository maintainers through the security contact or a private GitHub communication channel. Do not use a public issue for sensitive reports. Maintainers may remove content, restrict participation, or take other action they consider appropriate.

This code of conduct applies to project spaces and to interactions where someone is representing the Socializ community.
